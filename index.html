About Me

Bridging computation
& living systems

I am a final-year Biomedical Engineering student at Islamic University, Kushtia, with a research focus spanning computational cancer biology and functional biomaterials for wound healing. My work is driven by the belief that impactful biomedical solutions emerge where rigorous molecular understanding meets materials innovation.

My published research investigates N-Carbobenzoxy-L-Phenylalanine as a disruptor of ErbB2 homodimer signaling in breast cancer — combining molecular docking, ADMET profiling, and MD simulation to identify a promising therapeutic candidate.

My final-year project develops a Janus electrospun PCL/PCL-Gelatin bilayer membrane functionalized with caffeic acid — designed for pH-responsive colorimetric infection detection and dual antibiofilm-antioxidant activity in diabetic foot ulcer management. This represents the first reported integration of caffeic acid in PCL/gelatin electrospun systems in the literature.

I bring hands-on wet lab experience from an internship at HGRTC, Dhaka, clinical training in medical physics at the Bangladesh Atomic Energy Commission, and ongoing volunteer research at the Biomedical Research Center (Jahural Haque's Lab) at Islamic University — deepening my independent research capacity ahead of doctoral study.

Beyond the laboratory, I am a trained classical performing artist — a Nazrul Geeti singer and dancer holding First Class First certificates from Rajshahi Shilpakala Academy in both music and dance (4-year course). I performed as a listed artist at Bangladesh Betar for roughly four to five years. I also bring three years of teaching experience at Tinkers Technologies, an online EdTech platform, which has sharpened my ability to communicate complex ideas with clarity — a skill I carry into scientific writing and mentorship.

Current & Ongoing
Projects

★ Final Year Project · 2024–2025
Janus Electrospun PCL/PCL-Gelatin Bilayer Membrane Functionalized with Caffeic Acid for Diabetic Foot Ulcer Management

A novel asymmetric bilayer membrane fabricated by sequential electrospinning of PCL and PCL-Gelatin layers, functionalized with caffeic acid. The system leverages caffeic acid's pH-responsive anthocyanin-like colorimetric behavior to enable visual infection detection at the wound site, while simultaneously delivering dual antibiofilm and antioxidant activity. This represents the first reported use of caffeic acid in PCL/gelatin electrospun membranes in the literature.

Electrospinning PCL/Gelatin Caffeic Acid pH-Responsive Colorimetric Diabetic Wound Care Antibiofilm Antioxidant
🧬
Computational Targeting of ErbB2 Homodimer in Breast Cancer

Molecular docking, ADMET profiling, and molecular dynamics simulation to evaluate N-Carbobenzoxy-L-Phenylalanine as a disruptor of ErbB2 homodimer signaling — a validated driver of HER2-positive breast cancer progression.

Molecular Docking ADMET MD Simulation ErbB2/HER2

Clinical Medical Physics: PET-CT, LINAC & Radiopharmaceuticals

Structured clinical training at Bangladesh Atomic Energy Commission covering Philips Ingenuity TF PET-CT, cyclotron-based radiopharmaceutical production, Varian Clinac iX LINAC, and treatment planning systems (Eclipse/ARIA) including AAA and Acuros XB dose algorithms.
